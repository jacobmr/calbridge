For families

"Can you come over for dinner on the 15th?"

You don't know. Your partner's work calendar is on Outlook. Your kid's soccer is an ICS feed. Your school cal is somewhere else entirely. By the time you've checked everything, the window has closed.

MiCal merges every family calendar — across providers — into one shared view. Each parent sees what the other has on. School plays, soccer games, work meetings, dentist appointments — together. Answer "yes, we're free" with confidence.

  • Cross-platform sharing — Google, Outlook, iCloud (via ICS)
  • Color-coded by family member
  • Privacy you control — share full detail, or just free/busy
  • "Ask the family" — type a time, get an instant yes/no
For teams

"I'm the consultant juggling five clients."

Each client has their own Google Workspace or Office 365. Switching tabs to find your day is exhausting. Worse, getting double-booked because Client A can't see Client B's blocks.

MiCal connects them all. See every client's events in your personal calendar. Push "[Client A] busy" blocks back to their workspace so they don't book over you. Same feature works for agencies, partners, anyone with calendars across organizations.

  • One calendar to read, many to feed it
  • "[Name] " prefixes so it's always clear who's on the slot
  • Per-member rules — what they see, what gets pushed
  • Works for voice assistants — Alexa reads your unified day

Calendly and TidyCal own scheduling links. OneCal owns calendar sync. Cal.com is open-source booking. Reclaim is AI auto-scheduling. MiCal is the only one that does cross-provider sync, booking pages, and family/team schedules together — so the calendar your spouse, your client, and the soccer league all use can finally live in one place.
